#2 Why simply start a webpage isn't always the best idea

#html #css #anything

Of course you wanna start and you wanna work on the code, you just learned! That's ok for sure, the worst you can do is just to much think about. But in the retrospective of my homepage there are some things considering them first will save you a lot of time.

  • imagine your page first

What I mean by that is, that you should think about the design of your page first, then build it. The design you see right now is probably the fourth version of how the page looked in the beginning. By the way, if you want to see the evolution of this page you can check out my GitHub repository right here. There are several tools that can help you to imagine your result first. Figma, Sketch or Adobe XD just to name a few. Use these tools, u can simulate the behavoir of a webpage pretty well. With the right design of your page, this brings me to bulletpoint number 2:

  • which colors and fonts you want to use?

With the right design of your page there is no more question about fonts and colors. This is a thing I really underestimated. In the process of this page I had 6(!) different fonts and colors each. Thats way to much, also you got to keep in mind that a good design is as important as function. Finally:

  • use as few as possible classes and ids in your css file
