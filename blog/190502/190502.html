#3 What's your path on becoming a web-developer?
#javascript #html #css #roadmap

As I mentioned on my homepage before, it is really not easy to start to code. Why is this so? I would say there are two main reasons that reinforce this fact.

  1. education is way behind industry

I remember the first contact with computers at my school. The equipment was already oldschool back in the 90ies! But this was just hardware. You can 'update' your equipment by spending money (ok, that's easier said than done in the public sector!). But the real problem is knowledge. You have to keep up with the industry regarding standards and technology. This fact getting worse because as a teacher you have to be on top to tell the people what to learn. This can be the start for every new coder! Find your way through and you got your language! Maybe it's not that simple. I was a little surprised how few lines ended up in javascript! But at least it's the powerful underestimated (we are still in LotR...). Another good overview is from Sandeep Mehta who gives you an update of the The Ultimate 2019 Web Developer Roadmap. He splits his roadmap into two parts. First ones are the common coding skills, second one is the frontend section of web development. For the first part of course you need the three absolute basics javascript, html and css. Sandeep calls html and css as the 'lifeblood of web development'. Later on, for the second part of frontend web development, you got several choices which path you want to follow and also some recommended/must-have tools. This infographic gives you quite a good overview:

source: codeburst.io

As you can see the blue path are the basics. Later, you can choose between several orange branches. As he points out, main decision will later be which framework to choose. The three most known are React.js Angular and Vue.js. I will do another blog post about which framework to choose later. Interesting in Sandeep's article are the other tools he recommends to learn. Most important in the list (in my opinion) is git and github. Even working alone on a code is very convenient because you have a backup of your code, even your complete project. It gets even more convenient, if you use a origin like github. You also get versioning in your code, that means you can switch between different branches, developing a new feature for example, and you can get back to a earlier state of your code. But also the other topics in Sandeep's list will give you a general basis of web-development. Important of course other topics like HTTP/HTTPS, Data Structures and Algorithms. My personal choice is javascript, not only because it's easy to learn (regarding to the LotR overview), but also you got a bunch of possibilities to work on later.
