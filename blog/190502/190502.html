#3 What's your path on becoming a web-developer?

#javascript #html #css #framework #codeburst #roadmap



As I mentioned on my homepage (by the way, a lot of people always mix the two terms 'homepage' and 'webpage') before, it is really not easy to start to code. Why is this so? I would say there are two main reasons that reinforce this fact.




#1: education is way behind industry



I remember the first contact with computers at my school. The equipment was already oldschool back in the 90ies! But this was just hardware. You can 'update' your equipment by spending money (ok, that's easier said than done in the public sector!). But the real problem is knowledge. You have to keep up with the industry regarding standards and technology. This fact getting worse because as a teacher you have to be on top to tell the people what to learn. That's no easy job in the educational sector. In the private sector you got your boss, who tells you to implement the new technology by end of the week üë®‚Äçüíº‚òùÔ∏è. #2: this industry is HUUUUUUGE üë±‚Äç



You don't need bad hair to understand this. Starting with the language itself, the different libraries, frameworks, tools,...The 'problem' is that this industry is HUUU...ok enough... by itself, it also has a very rapid devolpment rate, espacially all things related javascript, in my opinion. No proof, but a note towards the rigth direction: just check the github octoverse, js is way ahead (...as is üëç)! A lot of repos on github is no proof, right, but it may be a good correlation between 'activity' and development in one language. Thank god the internet has a lot of information about this also! One article I already mentioned in my blog, but I have to mention it again, because it can help you a lot is this one from Quincy Larson.

But before I started my first lessons on freecodecamp.org There is one infographic that I really enjoyed! This picture from carlcheo.com gives you a first (and also funny) overview to the different programming languages and the analogy to Lord of the Rings:




source: carlcheo.com




This can be the start for every new coder! Find your way through and you got your language! Maybe it's not that simple. I was a little surprised how few lines ended up in javascript! But at least it's the powerful underestimated (we are still in LotR...).
Another good overview is from Sandeep Mehta who gives you an update of the The Ultimate 2019 Web Developer Roadmap. He splits his roadmap into two parts. First ones are the common coding skills, second one is the frontend section of web development. For the first part of course you need the three absolute basics javascript, html and css. Sandeep calls html and css as the 'lifeblood of web development'. Later on, for the second part of frontend web development, you got several choices which path you want to follow and also some recommended/must-have tools. This infographic gives you quite a good overview:


source: codeburst.io




As you can see the blue path are the basics. Later, you can choose between several orange branches. As he points out, main decision will later be which framework to choose. The three most known are React.js Angular and Vue.js. I will do another blog post about which framework to choose later. Interesting in Sandeep's article are the other tools he recommends to learn. Most important in the list (in my opinion) is .git and github. Even working alone on a code is very convenient because:

  1. backup

a backup of your code, even your complete project. It gets even more convenient, if you use a cloud remote git like github.


  1. versioning

you get versioning in your code, that means you can switch between different branches, developing a new feature for example, and you can get back to a earlier state of your code. (For a short overview I recommend this video from Traversy Media, he gives you a quick lesson about the most important git commands).


But also the other topics in Sandeep's list will give you a general basis of web-development. Important of course other topics like HTTP/HTTPS, Data Structures and Algorithms.
My personal choice is javascript, not only because it's easy to learn (regarding the LotR overview), but also you got a bunch of possibilities to work on later.
