#4 Complete rework of the page
#bootstrap #css #front-end library

It’s been a long time since my last blog contribution. I concentrated more on other projects, so not enough time for my page. Spent a lot of time for The Web Developer Bootcamp from Colt Steele. A really complete and comprehensive course, I really recommend for beginners like me! As you can see, I did a complete rework of my page using bootstrap 4. What a difference to vanilla css! When you start from scratch and you are not that experienced, you burn a lot of time correcting your css. One chapter of the Web Developer Bootcamp was completely about Bootstrap and how to apply for your own needs. When you understand the principles of container and row in bootstrap, you can simply adapt for your own needs. Completed by custom css, you can easily change classes and ids. And the best part of bootstrap is its complete ‘mobile first’ approach. In comparison: I needed roughly one month for the creation of my ‘old’ page. The bootstrap rework was done in 3 days! So anybody starting with a new page, give bootstrap a shot. Of course you need a basic knowledge of css, but especially changing padding and margin within a container, besides that all centered, it’s so easy with bootstrap! One warning by the way, bootstrap is no replacement for your css knowledge. In my opinion, the real strength of bootstrap comes in combination with your own css file. Bootstrap takes over the basic structure, the finishing touches come from your style.css.
